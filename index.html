📖 Lesson Overview

Today's lesson is all about your upcoming trip to England! We'll use this exciting topic to practice all three parts of the IELTS Academic Speaking test. You'll work on:

  • 🎯 Part 1: Answering short personal questions about your preparations and plans
  • 🎯 Part 2: Speaking for 2 minutes about a travel experience (with a real timer!)
  • 🎯 Part 3: Discussing bigger ideas about culture, tourism, and adapting abroad

🎮 How to use this lesson: Click on vocabulary cards to flip them, click "Show Prompts" to reveal hints, and use the timer for Part 2 practice!

⏱ 4–5 minutes

Part 1 – Introduction & Interview

Short, familiar questions about you, your trip, and your plans. Answer in 2–4 sentences.

💡 Speaking Tips for Your Trip & Beyond

🎤 Fluency Over Accuracy

Don't worry about making small mistakes. Focus on keeping the conversation going. Native speakers make mistakes too!

🧠 Buy Time Naturally

Use phrases like: "That's an interesting question...", "Let me think about that...", "Well, I'd say..."

📖 Extend Your Answers

Don't just say "yes" or "no". Always add: a reason, an example, or a personal story.

🔄 Paraphrase

If you forget a word, describe it! "The thing you use to..." is perfectly fine.

😊 Be Confident

Smile, make eye contact, and speak clearly. Your personality matters as much as your grammar!

🇬🇧 Real-Life Practice

In England, talk to locals! Ask for recommendations, order food, chat with your host. Every conversation is practice.

📝 Useful Phrases for the UK

"Cheers!" = Thank you (informal)
"Excuse me" = to get attention
"Sorry" = British people say this a lot!
"Lovely" = very nice

🎧 Listen Actively

If you don't understand, say: "Could you repeat that, please?" or "Sorry, I didn't catch that." It's completely okay!
